## Glossary of Key AI Terms (February 2025)

This glossary provides definitions for key terms frequently encountered in discussions about artificial intelligence. Note that the field is rapidly evolving, and some definitions may be refined over time.

* **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems. This includes learning, reasoning, and self-correction.  AI systems aim to mimic cognitive functions associated with human minds, such as learning and problem-solving.

* **Machine Learning (ML):** A subset of AI where systems learn from data without explicit programming. This involves algorithms that allow systems to identify patterns, make predictions, and improve their performance over time.  ML focuses on enabling systems to learn from experience rather than relying on pre-programmed rules.

* **Deep Learning (DL):** A more advanced subset of ML that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers to analyze data and extract complex features. This is particularly effective for image recognition, natural language processing, and other complex tasks. Deep learning models can learn intricate patterns and representations from data.

* **Generative AI:** AI models capable of creating new content, such as text, images, audio, and video. Examples include large language models (LLMs) and generative adversarial networks (GANs). Generative AI has revolutionized content creation across various domains.

* **Large Language Model (LLM):** A type of AI model trained on massive text datasets to perform tasks like text generation, translation, and question answering. Examples include GPT-3, LaMDA, and others. LLMs are capable of understanding and generating human-like text.

* **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** The ability of computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. This is crucial for applications like chatbots, machine translation, and sentiment analysis. NLP bridges the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

* **Computer Vision:** The ability of computers to "see" and interpret images and videos. This is used in applications like self-driving cars, medical imaging, and facial recognition. Computer vision enables machines to extract meaningful information from visual data.

* **Reinforcement Learning:** A type of ML where an agent learns to interact with an environment by receiving rewards and penalties. This is used in robotics, game playing, and other applications requiring decision-making. Reinforcement learning allows AI agents to learn optimal strategies through trial and error.

* **Explainable AI (XAI):** A branch of AI focused on making the decision-making processes of AI systems more transparent and understandable to humans. XAI addresses the "black box" problem of many AI models.

* **Bias in AI:** The tendency for AI systems to reflect and amplify biases present in their training data, leading to unfair or discriminatory outcomes. Addressing bias in AI is crucial for ensuring fairness and equity.
